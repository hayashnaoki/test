# Soba

### Open-Source Soba Project

**Project Title:** Scalable Soba

**Scope:** Cold or hot soba noodles as a reproducible, scalable system

---

## Concept

Soba is a system defined by ratio, time, and flow. Flour composition, hydration, rolling thickness, and boiling time determine quality. These parameters scale linearly, making soba suitable as an open, reproducible food systemâ€”small batch or large volume, same rules.

## Build Steps

1. **Define ratios**
    
    Set flour ratio (e.g. 80% buckwheat / 20% wheat). Scale quantities linearly.
    
2. **Mix dough**
    
    Gradually add water until dough holds together.
    
3. **Knead & rest**
    
    Knead until smooth. Rest briefly to stabilize structure.
    
4. **Roll**
    
    Roll dough to uniform thickness. Thickness directly controls cooking time and texture.
    
5. **Cut**
    
    Cut into consistent-width noodles. Precision improves scalability.
    
6. **Boil**
    
    Boil in large volumes of water. Batch size scales with pot capacity.
    
7. **Rinse / serve**
    
    Rinse for cold soba or transfer directly to broth for hot soba.
    
8. **Finish**
    
    Serve with tsuyu, toppings, and condiments.
    

---

## Scalability Notes

- Ingredient ratios scale linearly.
- Thickness and cut width are fixed parameters.
- Cooking time remains constant per noodle cross-section.
- Output volume is limited only by workspace and boiling capacity.
